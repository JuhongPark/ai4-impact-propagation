# Generative AI and LLMs in Marketing (Frontier Research)

How large language models and diffusion models are transforming creative production, personalization, and customer-facing marketing — the post-2023 wave that DD1-DD8 does not yet cover.

---

## 1. Why This Is a Frontier

Until 2022, "AI for marketing" primarily meant GNN-based recommendation, deep RL for bidding, Bayesian MMM, and causal ML for uplift. Since 2023, four forces have reshaped the field:

1. **Commoditized LLMs** (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama). Marketers now generate ad copy, email, and product descriptions in seconds.
2. **Production-ready diffusion models** (Stable Diffusion 3.5, FLUX, Midjourney). Custom ad imagery at near-zero marginal cost.
3. **Agentic AI** — autonomous agents that plan, act, and measure (see `agentic_ai_marketing.md` for that dimension).
4. **Mainstream adoption**: Generative AI marketing adoption roughly **doubled from 33% in 2023 to 71% in 2024**.

### The Hard Research Questions

It is trivially true that LLMs can generate ad copy. The real open problems are:

- **Brand-voice enforcement at scale**: How to guarantee 10,000 generated copies all sound like the same brand? Prompt templates, LoRA adapters, RAG-grounded style transfer, brand-specific instruction tuning.
- **Creative diversity vs. safety**: How to explore novel concepts while staying within brand-safety guardrails? A bandit-like exploration problem over the creative space.
- **Human-in-the-loop ratio**: What fraction of generated copy needs human review to preserve quality without killing throughput? An unsolved empirical question.
- **Evaluation at scale**: Human review doesn't scale to millions of ad variants. Learned quality predictors (CTR predictors, brand-voice classifiers) become the bottleneck.

## 3. Diffusion Models for Ad Creative

### Landmark Paper: Stable Diffusion for CTR-Optimized Ad Creative (ACM WebConf 2024)

- **Source**: ACM Web Conference 2024 Companion Proceedings ([paper](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3589335.3648315))
- **What it does**: Uses Stable Diffusion to generate candidate ad creatives conditioned on product attributes, then evaluates them through a click-through-rate predictor before serving.
- **Key idea**: Closes the loop between generative model and downstream CTR optimization — generate many candidates, filter via CTR predictor, serve the top.
- **Significance**: Moves diffusion from "pretty pictures" to "click-optimized ad creative." A production-relevant pipeline.

### Market Dominance and Economics

- **Stable Diffusion**: ~**80% market share** of AI-generated images (2024); **12.59B images** generated.
- **Stable Diffusion 3.5** (October 2024): 8.1B-parameter Large for enterprise; 2.5B Medium for consumer hardware.
- **Reported business impact**:
  - **−78% design cost** after diffusion integration (survey respondents)
  - **+60% engagement increase** on diffusion-generated creative
  - Gartner projection: **30% of outbound marketing messages** synthetically generated by 2025

### Consumer Reception Is Not Automatic

- **The Impact of Generative AI Images on Consumer Attitudes in Advertising** ([MDPI Administrative Sciences 2024](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3387/15/10/395)) — Empirical study of how consumers perceive AI-generated vs. human-made ad imagery. Finds meaningful consumer wariness. Disclosure and transparency requirements are emerging regulatory pressures.

This is not a solved problem. Consumer trust, disclosure norms, and platform-level labeling are still in flux.

## 4. Personalization at Scale

### The Shift

| Old personalization | New personalization |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| `Hi {first_name},` | Dynamic copy generated per user given full context |
| Rule-based segmentation | LLM-reasoned segmentation per query |
| Static creative per segment | Per-user creative rendered at request time |
| Last-touch attribution | Counterfactual causal attribution on generated content |

**73% of business leaders** believe AI will redefine personalization strategies ([Contentful 2026](https://www.contentful.com/blog/marketing-ai-digital-experiences-predictions-2026/)).

### Technical Enablers

1. **Prompt templates parameterized by user context**: LLM receives user attributes, session history, and a prompt template; produces copy at request time.
2. **Retrieval-augmented personalization**: LLM retrieves user-specific knowledge (purchase history, preferences) from a vector store and conditions generation on it.
3. **Real-time image generation**: Diffusion models generate per-user ad imagery — e.g., a product shown in the user's style, room, or context.
4. **Agentic orchestration** (see `agentic_ai_marketing.md`): An agent selects audiences, generates creative, allocates budget, and measures outcomes autonomously.

### The Governance Gap

Only **37% of marketers** include AI governance clauses in vendor contracts ([MarTech 2026](https://martech.org/ai-will-elevate-marketing/)). Most organizations lack formal safeguards for:

- Data quality failure and hallucination
- Lack of human oversight over content that reaches customers
- Brand safety violations
- Privacy and compliance violations (GDPR, CCPA, DSA)
- Algorithmic bias in targeting and content

This is the most important unsolved problem in the frontier: **scale without governance is disaster**.

## 5. Conversational Marketing via RAG

**Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** is the backbone of modern conversational commerce — the LLM grounds its answers in retrieved product catalog, inventory, and policy data rather than relying on parametric knowledge that decays.

### Retail-GPT (2024)

- **Paper**: [Retail-GPT: Leveraging RAG for Building E-commerce Chat Assistants](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.08925)
- **What it does**: Open-source RAG-based chatbot for retail e-commerce. Guides users through product discovery, recommends items, and assists with cart operations.
- **Architecture**:
  ```
  [Product Catalog + Policies]
    ↓ chunked + embedded
  [Vector Store]
    ↓ retrieval per query
  [LLM, grounded by retrieved context]
    ↓ tool use
  [Cart / checkout / recommendation output]
  ```
- **Significance**: End-to-end blueprint for RAG-based commerce **without** model fine-tuning. Applicable to any e-commerce catalog.

### Graph-Enhanced RAG for E-commerce Customer Support (2025)

- **Paper**: [Graph-Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented QA for E-commerce Customer Support](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.14267)
- **What it does**: Integrates **knowledge graphs** into RAG. Structured subgraphs are retrieved alongside unstructured documents.
- **Key insight**: Pure text RAG struggles with multi-hop queries like *"What's the return policy for items shipped to my address?"*. Graph + text hybrid resolves these.
- **Why it matters**: Establishes a template for commerce queries that require reasoning over structured business rules + unstructured policies simultaneously.

### Amazon Product-Aware Query Auto-Completion via RAG

- **Source**: Amazon Science ([PDF](https://assets.amazon.science/5a/e2/46450cad4f3cae1dbefd7b81deb8/a-product-aware-query-auto-completion-framework-for-e-commerce-search-via-retrieval-augmented-generation-method.pdf))
- **What it does**: Production RAG system for Amazon search that retrieves product-aware context to generate query completions.
- **Significance**: One of the few **production-scale** RAG deployments with a public writeup. Confirms RAG is the right pattern for e-commerce query and search completion at scale.

## 7. Open Problems and Future Directions

1. **Brand-voice fine-tuning at scale**: Production-ready techniques for injecting brand voice into LLM outputs. Instruction tuning per brand, LoRA adapters, or RAG-based style transfer.

2. **LLM + causal ML integration**: Personalization today uses LLMs for content and classical ML for targeting. Integrating causal uplift estimation directly with LLM content generation is an open frontier.

3. **Multi-modal generation with internal consistency**: Joint copy + image + video generation that is internally coherent. Current systems generate modalities separately and risk mismatch.

4. **Evaluation at scale**: Millions of generated creatives must be evaluated against CTR, conversion, and brand-safety criteria without human review of each. Learned evaluators are the bottleneck.

5. **Creator-brand-LLM economics**: LLMs and diffusion models commoditize creative generation, shifting the economic value of human creators. The creator economy is being rewritten.

6. **Generative MMM narratives**: Can LLMs read MMM output and automatically generate narrative explanations for CMOs? Or reverse — generate hypothetical scenarios for MMM counterfactual analysis? Both are unexplored.

7. **Disclosure and regulation**: As platforms mandate "made with AI" labeling, the effect on consumer engagement is an empirical question with major business consequences.
